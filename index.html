Story

We were introduced the traditional way—families, a few careful conversations, an engagement that felt more like a promise than a love story. At first, we were polite guests in each other’s lives: scheduled calls, thoughtful questions, gentle smiles.

Then the small things began to stack up. Her laugh on a late-night call. My bad jokes she pretended were good. Sharing tea, swapping stories, planning tiny pieces of a big future. We learned each other’s rhythms—how she thinks before she speaks, how I speak before I think—and met in the middle.

Somewhere between trading late-night voice notes and laughing over playlist choices, the arrangement quietly rewrote itself. We didn’t fall all at once; we chose each other, day by day—until choosing became love.
